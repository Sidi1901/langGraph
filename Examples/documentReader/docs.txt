A text splitter is an AI data pre-processing tool that divides large documents into smaller, meaningful chunks. It is essential for overcoming the context-window limits of Large Language Models (LLMs) and improving the accuracy of search-based AI systems (like RAG) by keeping text sizes uniform.Common text splitting methods include:Recursive Character Splitter: Intelligently breaks text by prioritizing larger boundaries (like paragraphs and sentences) before resorting to spaces.Token Splitter: Splits text strictly by token count (the basic units of text AI models process), ensuring chunks never exceed the model's input limit.Semantic Splitter: Analyzes the actual meaning and context of the text, grouping related sentences together rather than making arbitrary breaks.Markdown/Structured Splitter: Preserves formatting by splitting documents along headings, subheadings, and lists.
